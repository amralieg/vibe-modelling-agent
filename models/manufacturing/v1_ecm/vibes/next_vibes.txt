**Model Quality Score: 38/100**

**PRIORITY 1 — Workforce domain is a bloated god-domain acting as a proxy hub:** Over 80% of all FK relationships across the entire model route through workforce.* tables (account, employee, equipment, sales_order, component, etc.) rather than pointing to their true SSOT domains. For example, every domain references workforce.sales_order instead of order.sales_order, workforce.component instead of engineering.component, workforce.account instead of customer.account, workforce.billing_invoice instead of billing.invoice, workforce.procurement_supplier instead of procurement.supplier, workforce.equipment instead of asset.equipment, workforce.catalog_item instead of product.catalog_item, workforce.inventory_sku instead of inventory.sku, workforce.production_plant instead of production.plant, and workforce.warehouse instead of inventory.warehouse. Refactor all ~2,000+ misdirected FKs to point to the canonical domain owner, and demote workforce proxy tables to either views or remove them entirely, retaining in workforce only genuinely HR/labor entities (employee, job_position, org_unit, shift_definition, union_agreement, labor_classification, skill, certification, training_*, pay_period, payroll_result, performance_*, time_entry, absence_record, headcount_plan, etc.).

**PRIORITY 2 — Duplicate supplier tables across procurement domain:** procurement.supplier, procurement.supplier_performance, procurement.supplier_qualification, procurement.supplier_audit, procurement.supplier_contact, procurement.supplier_certificate, procurement.supplier_risk exist alongside procurement.procurement_supplier_performance, procurement.procurement_supplier_qualification, procurement.procurement_supplier_contact, procurement.procurement_supplier_invoice, and workforce.procurement_supplier. Consolidate each pair — remove the unprefixed stubs (procurement.supplier, procurement.supplier_performance, procurement.supplier_qualification, procurement.supplier_contact) and keep only the fully-attributed procurement.procurement_* versions, redirecting all FKs accordingly.

**PRIORITY 3 — Duplicate sourcing/purchasing tables in procurement:** procurement.sourcing_event vs procurement.procurement_sourcing_event, procurement.sourcing_bid vs procurement.procurement_sourcing_bid, procurement.spend_category vs procurement.procurement_spend_category, procurement.purchase_order vs workforce.procurement_purchase_order, procurement.purchase_requisition vs procurement.procurement_purchase_requisition, procurement.supplier_invoice vs procurement.procurement_supplier_invoice, procurement.goods_receipt vs procurement.procurement_goods_receipt, procurement.po_line_item vs procurement.procurement_po_line_item. Merge each pair into a single canonical table and update all FK references.

**PRIORITY 4 — Siloed and orphaned tables need FK connections:** The model reports 1 siloed table and 2 unlinked _id columns. Identify and connect these — likely candidates include quality.apqp_plan (zero inbound FKs except from quality.apqp_project), quality.defect_code (no inbound domain-level parent), billing.type (minimal context), and product.classification (only self-referencing via hierarchy). Add appropriate FKs to integrate them.

**PRIORITY 5 — Missing domain: Planning/S&OP:** Industrial manufacturing requires a dedicated Sales & Operations Planning / Supply Chain Planning domain covering demand planning, supply planning, capacity planning, S&OP consensus plans, and inventory optimization. Currently MRP lives in procurement and capacity in production with no unified planning orchestration. Add a planning domain with tables for demand_plan, supply_plan, sop_cycle, consensus_forecast, and capacity_plan.

**PRIORITY 6 — Missing domain: Master Data / Organization:** Core organizational entities (legal_entity, business_unit, cost_center, profit_center, production_plant, functional_location, warehouse) are scattered across workforce and finance. Create an organization domain to serve as the true SSOT for organizational hierarchy and plant/site master data, and redirect all FKs from workforce.legal_entity, workforce.production_plant, workforce.cost_center, workforce.profit_center, workforce.functional_location, workforce.warehouse, workforce.business_unit.

**PRIORITY 7 — order domain missing a proper sales_order table:** The order domain has no sales_order product — it lives in workforce.sales_order. This means the order domain (described as SSOT for customer orders) cannot fulfill its stated purpose. Move sales_order into the order domain and redirect all ~50+ FK references from workforce.sales_order.

**PRIORITY 8 — asset.class is a stub product with no FKs and a generic name:** asset.class has no outbound FKs and serves only as a lookup. Rename to asset.equipment_class or asset.asset_class to avoid SQL reserved-word conflicts and ambiguity, and add an FK to product.category to link asset classification to the product hierarchy.

**PRIORITY 9 — billing.type is a stub product with no FKs:** billing.type has zero outbound FKs and appears to be a simple lookup that was promoted to a full table. Either enrich it with FKs to billing.plan or product.category, or demote it to an enum/reference attribute on billing tables that reference it.

**PRIORITY 10 — Missing FK: production.batch to engineering.engineering_bom:** Production batches are manufactured from BOMs but production.batch has no FK to engineering.engineering_bom or production.version's BOM reference. Add a direct FK from production.batch to engineering.engineering_bom to enable BOM traceability per batch.

**PRIORITY 11 — Missing FK: service.warranty_claim to billing.invoice:** Warranty claims that result in credit or billing adjustments have no direct link to billing. Add FK from service.warranty_claim to billing.credit_note and/or workforce.billing_invoice (or the corrected billing.invoice) to close the financial loop.

**PRIORITY 12 — Missing FK: logistics.delivery to order.line_item and order.schedule_line:** Deliveries fulfill order lines and schedule lines, but logistics.delivery has no FK to order.line_item or order.schedule_line — only to workforce.sales_order via workforce.shipment. Add direct FKs from logistics.delivery to order.line_item and order.schedule_line for proper order fulfillment traceability.

**PRIORITY 13 — Suspicious self-referencing FKs:** technology.configuration_item FK to technology.configuration_item, technology.it_vendor FK to technology.it_vendor, technology.it_service FK to technology.it_service, technology.standard FK to technology.standard, workforce.business_unit FK to workforce.business_unit, and workforce.it_asset FK to workforce.it_asset all need verification that they carry a semantically distinct column name (e.g., parent_configuration_item_id). If any use the bare PK name as the FK, they are invalid self-joins and must be corrected with a labeled prefix like parent_ or replaces_.

**PRIORITY 14 — Naming inconsistency across procurement tables:** The procurement domain mixes two naming conventions — bare names (supplier, goods_receipt, po_line_item, sourcing_event) and prefixed names (procurement_supplier_invoice, procurement_goods_receipt, procurement_po_line_item, procurement_sourcing_event). After deduplication per Priority 3, standardize all remaining tables to use the bare snake_case convention without the domain prefix, consistent with every other domain's naming pattern.

**PRIORITY 15 — Missing FK: customer.installed_base to service.service_contract and service.entitlement:** The installed base is the anchor for aftermarket service, yet customer.installed_base has no FK to any service contract or entitlement table. Add FKs from customer.installed_base to service.entitlement and asset.service_contract to enable installed-base-driven service coverage queries.
